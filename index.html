Ocean Conditions

· nearshore waves combined with marine wind

Wind works on the water two ways. Out at sea, over hours and miles, it builds the waves you see here; the buoy measures the finished result, so wave size already reflects the wind that made it. Right at the bluff, local wind grooms the surface, smoothing or roughening it without changing the wave size.

How it rides right now · the feel out on the water
—
Steepness — but not the everyday kind: not how tall the waves are, but how tall they stand against how far apart they run. Long, well-spaced waves ride easy; the same height packed close rides hard. Oceanographers call this wave steepness (wave height divided by wavelength).
